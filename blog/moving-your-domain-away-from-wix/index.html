Moving Your Domain Away From Wix – 3 Simple Steps

By Reza Jalaei· Oct 14, 2022· 3 min read

  • Domains
  • Tech
  • Wix

A few years ago I came across a great a deal on Wix which contained a free domain, unfortunately then I got trapped into their ecosystem of slow load times and constant price hikes.

Last week I decided to cancel my plan — after they increase the price by 250% — after a quick search I came across two alternatives, IONOS and NameCheap.

Regarding price they were both offering free transfers along with a required $10 one-year extension.

The minimum one-year extension is required for more TLDs.

I first tried IONOS but after paying the fee and waiting for two days for a confirmation email, I got in touch with their support and discovered an “unknown issue with my domain contact information” which caused the transfer to fail. I’m still waiting for the refund.

After that I initiated the transfer to NameCheap.

The first step is to unlock the domain from Wix, to do so:

Login to your Wix dashboard and navigate to Domains from the menu

Click on … next to the domain you wish to transfer away and the Transfer away from Wix.

A few popups will open to dissuade from the transfer, offering discounts and bogus warning regarding your DNS, ignore them and continue.

After a few minutes you’ll receive a code in your inbox with the transfer code.

It’s not possible to initiate the transfer without this code. The code goes to the email used to register the domain (not your Wix account email — incase that’s different find your registration email from Edit contact info).

Now with the code you can head over to NameCheap Domain Transfer and start the transfer.

At the time of this post, NameCheap is offer a $9.78 deal for .com transfers which includes a one-year extension of your domain.

Keep in mind that after the second year the rate increases to $14.58.

After typing in your domain name, you’ll need to provide the transfer authorization code which was sent to your inbox earlier.

Then comes the payment and you’re done.

The actual transfer process takes anywhere from 48 hours to 7 days, in case of Wix it seems like the transfer always takes 7 days.

Never the less you will receive an email from opensrs.org after a day — this is the actual registrar that Wix uses to register domain — to inform you about the transfer initiation which also contains the timeframe for your transfer.

I’m still waiting for my transfer to finish but so far the process has been smooth.

A bonus was a message I received from the CEO of NameCheap thanking me for transfering my domain to NameCheap. Of course it was automated but still a nice touch!

A few notes:

ICANN, the nonprofit organization that is the authority for .com (and many other TLDs) transfers has certain rules regarding transfers, most of them come down to recent changes to your domain.

If you have already transferred, or edited your contact information in the last 60 days you won’t be able to initiate a transfer to a new registrar.
